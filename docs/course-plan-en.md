# Detailed Course Plan (English)

## Step 1: Getting Started (A1.1)

| Lesson | Focus | Theory Highlights | Key Vocabulary | Practice | Goal |
|--------|-------|-------------------|----------------|----------|------|
| Lesson 1: Greetings and Introductions | Greetings, introductions | Latin alphabet; greetings and polite expressions; verb **biti** (to be) | dobro jutro, dobar dan, doviđenja, hvala, molim | Matching phrases; fill-in-the-blank; short dialogues | Introduce yourself; greet and say goodbye |
| Lesson 2: At the Café | Ordering food/drinks | Verb **imati** (to have); polite ordering; noun gender | kafa, voda, pivo, čaj, hrana | Ordering patterns ("Ja bih kafu."); choosing drinks; waiter–guest dialogues | Order food or drinks; ask for the bill; express thanks |
| Lesson 3: Learning Serbian | Study vocabulary and verbs | Present tense conjugation (-ti → -m/-š/-ju); verbs učiti, čitati, raditi | učiti, govoriti, razumeti, škola, novine | Conjugation drills; short texts; yes/no questions ("Da li…?") | Talk about studying Serbian; describe understanding and reading |
| Lesson 4: Where is...? | Directions & locations | Prepositions **u**, **na** + locative case; question "Gde je...?" | ulica, trg, banka, restoran, levo, desno, pravo | City map exercises; giving directions; "Go straight / Turn left" | Ask for and give directions |
| Lesson 5: In the Room / Hotel | Rooms, objects, permission | Verb **moći** (can); adjective agreement by gender; prepositions **na**, **ispred**, **pored** | soba, sto, stolica, krevet, orman, sprat | Describing a room; locating objects; numbers practice | Describe spaces; ask about everyday objects |
| Lesson 6: Shopping for Food | Groceries and quantities | Quantities (kilo, litar); expressions **treba mi**, **želim** | voće, povrće, hleb, sir, mleko, cena | Shopping dialogues ("Dajte mi…"); asking for prices | Buy groceries; ask for amounts; thank the seller |
| Lesson 7: Come to My Place | Invitations and scheduling | Days of the week; time expressions; verbs **voleti**, **hteti** | dan, vreme, sastanak, gosti | Arranging meetings; talking about availability | Discuss your schedule; invite someone |

## Step 2: Everyday Life (A2.1)

| Lesson | Focus | Theory Highlights | Key Vocabulary | Practice | Goal |
|--------|-------|-------------------|----------------|----------|------|
| Lesson 8: Family and Friends | Family relationships | Plural of nouns; possessive adjectives | otac, majka, brat, sestra, prijatelj | Building a family tree; using moj/tvoj/naš/vaš | Talk about family and relationships |
| Lesson 9: Daily Routine | Habits and routines | Reflexive verbs (se buditi, se umivati); genitive singular | jutro, veče, navike, aktivnosti | Describing daily activities; sequencing tasks | Describe daily habits and routines |
| Lesson 10: People and Descriptions | Appearance and personality | Adjective agreement; question **kakav/kakva/kakvo** | visok, lep, zanimljiv, ljubazan | Matching descriptions; describing friends | Describe appearance and personality |
| Lesson 11: Nationalities and Languages | Countries and languages | Locative case; adjectives from country names; question "Odakle si?" | Srbija, Crna Gora, Rusija, Engleska, srpski, crnogorski | Conversations about origin and language | Say where you are from and what languages you speak |

## Step 3: Expanding Communication (A2.2)

| Lesson | Focus | Theory Highlights | Key Vocabulary | Practice | Goal |
|--------|-------|-------------------|----------------|----------|------|
| Lesson 12: Weather and Seasons | Weather and climate | Instrumental case; seasons; weather adjectives | sunčano, kiša, sneg, hladno, leto, zima | Weather forecast dialogues; comparing seasons | Describe weather and time of year |
| Lesson 13: Travel and Transport | Travel planning | Instrumental plural; prepositions **sa**, **bez**, **između** | autobus, voz, avion, putovanje | Planning trips; station dialogues | Talk about travel and transportation |
| Lesson 14: Health and Body | Health vocabulary | Verb **boleti** (to hurt); body parts; imperative mood | glava, ruka, bol, doktor, tableta | Describing symptoms; pharmacy dialogues | Talk about health and ask for help |

## Step 4: Real-Life Interaction (B1.1)

| Lesson | Focus | Theory Highlights | Key Vocabulary | Practice | Goal |
|--------|-------|-------------------|----------------|----------|------|
| Lesson 15: Jobs and Professions | Work and occupations | Dative case; profession vocabulary; work verbs | lekar, učitelj, inženjer, raditi | Filling a short CV; work conversations | Talk about work and occupation |
| Lesson 16: Hobbies and Free Time | Interests | Verbs **voleti**, **baviti se**; infinitive structures | sport, muzika, čitanje, film | Preference surveys; "Šta voliš da radiš?" dialogues | Discuss hobbies and interests |
| Lesson 17: Future Plans | Planning ahead | Future tense (ću + infinitive); time expressions | sutra, sledeće godine, planovi | Talking about future plans; schedules | Express intentions and plans |
| Lesson 18: Comparisons and Opinions | Comparing and evaluating | Degrees of comparison; expressing opinions (**mislim da...**) | bolji, najbolji, lošiji, mišljenje | Comparing items/people; choosing preferences | Express opinions and comparisons |
| Lesson 19: Review & Storytelling | Integrated skills | Verb aspect (perfective vs. imperfective); narrative usage | — | Writing a short story; tense review | Speak more freely in everyday situations |
